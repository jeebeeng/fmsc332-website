Home Safety for Children Aged 5 Years and Less

At this precious stage of life, it's paramount to create a home environment that nurtures the curiosity of your bundle of joy while prioritizing their safety. Babies are naturally explorers, and as parents, it's our responsibility to be vigilant guardians of their well-being. Household hazards lurk in unsuspecting corners, from sharp edges to unsecured cabinets, and electrical outlets, posing potential risks to your little adventurer. Each year, ther are about two million children under the age of five who are sent to emergency rooms for injuries that occur in the home (Baby Proofers Plus).
Our mission is to empower you with knowledge and practical solutions to identify and eliminate these hazards, ensuring your home becomes a haven of security for your growing family. Together, let's embark on a journey of awareness, adopting preventive measures that promote a safe and nurturing environment for your baby's developmental milestones. Because when it comes to your little one, there's no compromise on safety.

Household Hazards

There are many hazards within a home, some of which include:

Hazards for falls
No gates at stairs, windows that open beyond 15cm
Hazards for poisonings and chokings
Choking hazards within child's reach, easily accessible household cleaning supplies
Hazards for burns
Tap water temperature greater than 54 degrees celcius, chords dangling from appliances in kitchen, unattended stove, matches or lighters within child's reach
Hazards for drowning
Lack of supervision in areas with water (bathtub, pool), easy access to areas with water
Finger entrapments
Children can get their fingers stuck in small holes or slots
For babies under the age of 1, although they may not have the motor skills to be out and about, there are still dangers within the crib of the baby
There are also hazards within the baby's crib, such as defects in the product, stuffed animals, and blankets
Pillows and blankets are frequently associated with unintentional suffocation and stragulation of infants (Gaw, 2017)

Prevention

Hazards for falls
Place gates at the top and bottom of stairs to prevent babies from falling down the stairs
Always keep windows closed and locked to prevent any chance of the baby falling through the window
Hazards for poisonings and chokings
Keep all household products, medication, bathroom products, paint, along with some plants out of reach of your child
Use child-resistant lids on household products and containers
Keep small, ingestible objects out of baby's reach
Hazards for burns
Use the back of the stove to cook and keep pot and pan handles towards the back of the stove
Make sure smoke detectors are functional
Use a stove guard to prevent child from grabbing pots
Keep matches and lighters out of child's reach
Keep your hot water heater set below 54 degrees celcius to prevent scalding
Hazards for drowning
Never leave a child unattended around water
Have a cover and childproof fence to keep children out of these areas
Finger entrapments
Ensure that openings are larger than 0.375-inch diameter or smaller tan a 0.210-inch diameter
Keep the baby's crib free of stuffed animals, blankets, bumper pads, and positioning wedges
A baby's crib may also arrive with defects. Make sure to check reviews and if there have been any recalls of the product before purchasing
